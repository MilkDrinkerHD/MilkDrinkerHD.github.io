IT Jobs in 2040 – A Look at the Future of IT

Presentation by Hugh R and Carson A

Overview

As technology evolves, so do the careers that support it. This presentation reviews changes from 2010–2025 and extrapolates to 2040, considering AI, cloud reliance, and automation.

Timeline

  • 2010 → 2025: Cloud, mobile, DevOps, remote collaboration, cybersecurity demand surged.
  • 2030–2035: Regulation on AI safety/ethics grows, increasing oversight roles.
  • 2040: Bio‑IT convergence and quantum shifts reshape security and systems.

IT Roles: 2010 vs 2025

Jobs expanded and specialized while retaining core themes (e.g., sysadmin → DevOps/SRE; on‑prem → cloud-native).

Hardware Evolution (2010–2025)

  • 2010: In‑house servers, HDDs, local desktops
  • 2015: SSDs, virtualization‑ready CPUs, efficient data centers
  • 2020: Cloud hardware explosion, GPUs/ARM/IoT everywhere
  • 2025: AI acceleration, near‑complete virtualization

Software Evolution (2010–2025)

  • 2010: Standalone desktop apps, manual installs, infrequent updates
  • 2015: SaaS adoption, version control, faster workflows
  • 2020: Remote collaboration, containerization, digitized finance
  • 2025: AI‑integrated tools, no‑code, autonomous programming systems

Salary Evolution (2010–2025)

Degree‑required IT roles rose from ~$50–$70k in 2010; cloud, DevOps, security, and data engineering pushed many roles well into six figures by 2025; AI‑adjacent roles accelerated demand.

Drivers of Change

  • 2012 smartphone app boom
  • Upload speed increases enabling cloud adoption
  • 2020 remote‑work surge
  • 2023 AI explosion

Into the Future

  • Quantum computing → post‑quantum crypto transition and emergency hardening
  • AI as partner, not just tool
  • Ubiquitous high‑speed internet → decentralization
  • Bio‑IT convergence; AI safety compliance roles

New Jobs in 2040

  • Digital Forensic Analysts for AI‑generated content
  • Bio‑Interface Technicians
  • AI Safety Compliance Officers
  • Virtual Identity & Finance Verification Specialists

Mainstays

  • Physical network administration & connectivity maintenance
  • Human IT support (possibly premium)
  • System administrators
  • Project managers

Salaries in the Future

Security specialists likely see spikes during post‑quantum transitions; overall IT remains a comfortable, high‑demand field if skills keep pace.

Conclusion

Despite automation, total IT employment likely grows. Foundational roles remain while workflows and job scopes shift. Continuous learning stays essential; human support may become a premium experience.
